**Target Product Lines:** Skyfold, Modernfold, Euro-Wall, Airolite, Smoke Guard  
Line Spacing: 1.15

## **2\. Comprehensive Preconstruction Workflows**

### **Skyfold (Vertical Retractable Walls)**

* **Structural Verification:** Force the structural engineer to explicitly sign off on the dead/live loads of the overhead steel frame. Deflection tolerances must not exceed manufacturer guidelines (typically zero deflection under full load).  
* **Plenum Management:** Execute a coordination meeting with HVAC and plumbing subs. Map a dedicated "no-fly zone" inside the ceiling cavity along the entire length of the wall footprint.  
* **Electrical Supply:** Coordinate high-voltage lines (3-phase 208V/480V) with the electrical contractor. Document the exact disconnect switch locations on the submittal drawings.

### **Modernfold (Horizontal Operable Partitions)**

* **Pocket/Closet Architecture:** Verify that the layout for the stacking pocket matches the calculated stack depth (Panel Thickness × Number of Panels \+ Manufacturer Clearances). Ensure no electrical panels or switches are located within this perimeter.  
* **Header Support:** Ensure structural backing can sustain heavy concentrated loads when all panels are stacked into the closet area.  
* **Acoustic Continuity:** Issue a formal notice to the GC that an acoustic plenum barrier (baffle) must be constructed directly above the track line to block sound from transferring through the ceiling grid.

### **Euro-Wall (Large-Panel Glass Systems)**

* **Sill Integration:** For interior applications, ensure the concrete subfloor has an engineered recess/trench to achieve a flush track profile. For exterior builds, coordinate the drainage trough detail.  
* **Header Rigidity:** Verify structural header deflection limits do not exceed L/720 under full glass weight to eliminate panel binding.  
* **Code Compliance:** Validate wind-load and impact engineering values against local structural mandates (e.g., Florida Product Approval numbers).

### **Airolite (Architectural Louvers)**

* **Airflow Validation:** Match the "Free Area" geometry of the louvers against the HVAC air-handling CFM metrics. Alterations to blade styles can starve mechanical equipment.  
* **Waterproofing Intersect:** Define structural buck lines and ensure perimeter vapor barriers and sill pan flashing are deployed prior to louver anchoring.

### **Smoke Guard (Deployable Fire Curtains)**

* **FACP Relay Integration:** Issue low-voltage schematic diagrams to the fire alarm contractor to establish a fail-safe deployment loop.  
* **Power Continuity:** Secure a dedicated 120V AC circuit to run the integrated emergency battery backups.

## **3\. Comprehensive Master Field Compliance Matrix**

Utilize this targeted checklist during "Measuring Day" to identify structural anomalies and trade issues before releasing orders to fabrication.

| Category | Specific Non-Compliance Item to Inspect | Target Tolerances & Conditions | Impact of Non-Compliance   |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Structural & Framing** | Overhead Support Steel Levelness (Skyfold/Modernfold) | Flat within ±1/8 inch over the entire horizontal span. | Causes the mechanism to bind; prevents vertical systems from sealing against the floor. |
|  | Drywall Pocket / Closet Depth & Width (Modernfold) | Must match calculated stack width \+ manufacturing clearance lines. | Panels will protrude into the main room, preventing pocket door closure. |
|  | Light-Gauge Drywall Headers (Interior Euro-Wall) | Must utilize heavy timber blocking or structural steel tubes. | Severe header sag under glass load; forces doors to pinch and lock up. |
| **Plumb & Level (Alignment)** | Side Wall Plumbness / Bowing (All Products) | Strictly vertical, plumb within 1/8 inch over total opening height. | Causes gaps in perimeter acoustic or weather seals; bows Smoke Guard track rails. |
|  | Floor Levelness / Slab Crowning (Modernfold/Euro-Wall) | Variance less than 1/4 inch across travel footprint. | Bottom drop-down seals fail to block sound; doors scrape high spots. |
|  | Opening Squareness (Euro-Wall/Airolite) | Diagonal cross-measurements must match exactly. | Rectangular panels will not align or function inside a parallelogram opening. |
| **Mechanical & Plumbing** | Plenum Obstructions (Skyfold/Modernfold) | Clear, empty space inside the structural pocket zone. | Ductwork or conduit paths block the path of retractable vertical panels. |
|  | Depth Penetration (Airolite) | Wall cavity depth must accommodate louver frame width (e.g., 4" or 6"). | Louver strikes interior mechanical lines, plumbing pipes, or structural pillars. |
|  | Wall Face Obstructions (Smoke Guard) | Clear surface directly above and adjacent to elevator frames. | Exit signs, fire sprinkler heads, or HVAC diffusers impede curtain tracks. |
| **Electrical & Systems** | High-Voltage Power Drops (Skyfold) | Correct 3-phase voltage routed to designated disconnect switch site. | Inability to commission motors; delayed electrical sign-offs. |
|  | Dedicated 120V Circuitry (Smoke Guard) | Continuous AC source delivered to housing control enclosure. | System cannot maintain emergency battery configurations. |
|  | Fire Alarm Control Interface (Smoke Guard) | Programmed low-voltage relay terminated inside the safety module. | Curtain fails to deploy upon building fire alarm activation. |

## **4\. Communication Matrix & Project Schedule Milestones**

To operate effectively, frame the project timeline to the General Contractor around clear architectural and physical triggers rather than calendar dates alone.

1. **The Project Script:** *"The fabrication lead time is \[X\] weeks. This duration does not begin upon contract signature; it initiates exclusively when shop drawings are fully approved, finishes are finalized, and field conditions are certified as compliant. If framing or structural steel installations slip, our delivery timeline shifts accordingly."*  
2. **Site Protection Mandate:** Inform the GC that specialized floor-protection materials (e.g., Masonite or 3/4-inch plywood sheets) and non-marking wheel covers (diapers) must be on-site if scissor lifts are forced to travel over finalized flooring materials.  
3. **System Sign-off Strategy:** Require a formal walkthrough with the GC Superintendent immediately upon system installation. Document functional operations and secure signature acceptance to isolate your firm from damage caused by subsequent interior trades.

## **5\. Sales & Estimating Framework**

### **Critical Cost Drivers**

* **Material & Factory Escalation:** Account for long sales cycles (6 to 18 months). Include a 3% to 5% escalation buffer or explicitly state pricing expiration to handle factory surcharges and freight spikes.  
* **Specialized Equipment & Mobilization Logistics:** Budget explicitly for crane rentals, glass suction-cup rigs, or manual hoisting for upper levels. Account for multi-phase mobilizations (e.g., track phase vs. panel phase).  
* **Labor Complexity:** Factor in factory-certified labor rates and analyze local contract requirements for prevailing wage or union mandates.

### **Bid Packet Requirements**

* **Scope of Work:** Document specific model numbers, geometric constraints, STC ratings, finishes, and exact glass types.  
* **Explicit Exclusions:** Protect execution margins by formally excluding structural steel welding, high/low voltage electrical drops, final drywall cavity finishing, perimeter waterproofing/caulking, and floor protection.  
* **Clarifications & Value Engineering (VE):** Deliver pre-quoted aesthetic options and finish variances to simplify the procurement options for the General Contractor.

## **6\. Sales-to-PM Handoff & The "Red Line Review"**

To ensure a frictionless transaction from sales to construction management, teams must execute a mandatory 30-minute **Red Line Review** prior to signature authorization.

* **Contract Audit:** The Sales Representative and Project Manager review the contract draft to identify hidden "scope creep" clauses sneaky GCs add (e.g., forcing the subcontractor to supply perimeter joint sealants or scaffolding).  
* **Elimination of Assumptions:** Strike out non-compliant scope additions prior to contract closing. Hand the PM a vetted folder containing finalized submittal chips, targeted mechanical sheets (M-Series drawings), and signed load data.

## **7\. Advanced Product Nuances**

* **The Gravity Fail-Safe & The "AHJ" (Smoke Guard):** Curtains deploy automatically via mechanical weight if both AC power and emergency batteries drop out completely. Proactive alignment with the Authority Having Jurisdiction (AHJ / Fire Marshal) prevents Certificate of Occupancy hold-ups.  
* **Value Engineering (VE) Stacking (Modernfold vs. Skyfold):** Capitalize on structural budget shortfalls by proposing high-end horizontal tracking partitions (Modernfold) as a budget-saving structural alternative to vertical ceiling-retracting platforms (Skyfold).  
* **The "Glass Lead Time" Trap (Euro-Wall):** Glass manufacturing (especially large-format laminated impact selections) runs on distinct, highly fluctuating supply lines compared to standard frame extrusion. Confirm individual glass mill timelines before establishing hard site deadlines.  
* **The "Paint Cover" & Safety Labeling Risk:** Hurried painting and stucco trades frequently coat over warning stickers and critical UL Fire Rating Labels. Mask off factory markings comprehensively to ensure swift structural code sign-offs.
